TRADITIONAL DOBUK

The traditional dobok is the standard uniform worn for general training and practice in Taekwondo. It encompasses the fundamental elements of Taekwondo attire and is versatile for various training activities.

Traditional doboks for both male and female practitioners typically consist of a white jacket (jeogori) and white pants (baji), along with a belt (ti) that signifies the practitioner's rank. While some schools or organizations may have specific preferences for the design of traditional doboks, the basic structure remains consistent. These doboks prioritize comfort, durability, and ease of movement for activities such as basic techniques, forms practice, and conditioning drills. The white color symbolizes purity, humility, and the beginner's mindset in Taekwondo training.

POOMSAE DOBUK

The poomsae dobok is tailored for practicing and performing poomsae, the predetermined sequences of movements in Taekwondo. It's designed to allow for freedom of movement and enhance the aesthetics of the practitioner's techniques.

The primary difference between male and female poomsae doboks is often in the color of the pants. Male poomsae doboks typically feature black pants, while female poomsae doboks often have blue pants. This variation is primarily for aesthetic purposes and to distinguish between genders during competitions or demonstrations. Otherwise, the design and construction of the doboks remain similar for both male and female practitioners.

SPARRING DOBUK

The sparring dobok is intended for use during Taekwondo sparring matches or competitions. It's designed to provide both mobility and protection to the practitioner during intense combat situations.

Sparring doboks may be differentiated by color to distinguish between competitors during matches. Male sparring doboks often come in blue, while female sparring doboks are typically red. This color contrast helps referees and judges identify competitors and assign points accurately during sparring competitions. The doboks themselves are crafted to be durable, breathable, and provide some level of protection without restricting movement.
